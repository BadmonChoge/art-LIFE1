Research & Advocacy

The Art-Life Research & Advocacy Program conducts regular studies to evaluate the role and impact of the arts across Nairobi and beyond. We gather insightful data to drive evidence-based industry reforms and strengthen the creative economy.

Academic Partnership

In partnership with the University of Nairobi (Faculty of Arts & Social Sciences), we conduct rigorous research that informs policy, supports artists, and strengthens Kenya's creative industries through evidence-based insights.

Research

Our research initiatives provide critical insights into the role and impact of arts in society, helping artists align their work with public expectations and supporting evidence-based policy making.

Key Research Initiatives:

  • 365 Art-Life Statistics: National opinion poll asking "Why do the arts matter?"
  • Art-Life Facts: Demographic and media-sourced community data
  • Real-time public insight collection
  • Baseline intelligence on arts impact
  • Skills gap and opportunity analysis

Research Impact:

  • Helps artists align work with public expectations
  • Supports cultural advocates with data for policy engagement
  • Informs government and institutions on arts value
  • Guides dialogue, advocacy, and funding strategies
  • Highlights skills gaps for capacity-building

Advocacy

At Art-Life, we believe anyone can be a voice for the arts. Through training, data, and public programs, we equip creatives and community members to become bold advocates for a vibrant and inclusive cultural future.

Advocacy Focus:

  • Regular engagement with policymakers and arts leaders
  • Training programs for community advocates
  • Public awareness campaigns
  • Policy reform initiatives
  • Arts industry representation

Advocacy Impact:

  • Arts foster civic pride, dialogue, healing, and innovation
  • Creative industries contribute to employment and local economies
  • Investing in arts strengthens tourism and education
  • Promotes social cohesion and community building
  • Ensures artists' voices are heard and valued
